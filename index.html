Two candidates — Rise Beyond Barriers (RBB) & Education Lights the Path (ELP)
March 2026 · Confidential
Context
Why we're considering a new name
There is an existing NGO called Education Beyond Borders (EBB) which can cause confusion with our identity. While our core mission — removing barriers (not borders) in the way of girls' education — remains unchanged, we want a name that is uniquely ours and immediately recognizable on the international stage.
2 Name Proposals
Option A
RBB
Rise Beyond Barriers
The name speaks directly to our mission — removing the barriers that stand between girls and their education. "Rise" conveys upward movement, agency, and empowerment.
Why it works
✦ "BB" retained from EBB → easy brand transition
✦ Action-oriented, empowering tone
✦ Clear & direct on an international stage
✦ Slogan: "One step at a time, one life at a time"
Option B
ELP
Education Lights the Path
A poetic and evocative name — education is not just a tool, it is the light that illuminates the way forward. The metaphor resonates across cultures and languages.
Why it works
✦ "Education" front and centre
✦ Hopeful, poetic — memorable globally
✦ Completely new direction from EBB
✦ Slogan: "Every girl deserves to find her way"

Why Redesign the Logo
Current Logo
Issues with the Current Logo
  • Too many elements — globe, book, figure, dotted lines, pins — becomes illegible at small sizes
  • 5+ colors used with no clear brand color
Why the World's Most Recognized Brands Keep Their Logos Simple
1 color (red)
→ Instantly recognized at any size
1 color (black or white)
→ Recognized worldwide without any explanation
1 color (blue)
→ Trusted by donors in 190+ countries
Logo Design Principle
A great logo uses 1–2 colors and one symbol. It must read clearly on a business card, website and social media — and communicate the brand's core value without any explanation.

We should also consider how it looks on physical assets — such as stickers on laptops, and prints on T-shirts.

Color Usage Guide
🟢 Forest Green — Main
Logo background, headers, primary buttons, navbar, footer background
🟡 Bright Yellow — Point
Logo mark, CTA buttons, highlights, key statistics, accent borders
🤎 Warm Brown — Supporting
Body text on light backgrounds, secondary headings, subtle accents
🟤 Warm Cream — Background
Page background, card backgrounds, gives warmth vs. cold white
🌿 Mid Green — Secondary
Success states, progress indicators, secondary icons, hover states
⬜ White — On Dark BG
Body text on green backgrounds, secondary logo version
